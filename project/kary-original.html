About

About

I’m a UC Irvine student pursuing a B.S. in Computer Science and a B.A. in Psychological Science, and I’m part of the ICS Honors Program.

My work is shaped by both sides of my background: computer science teaches me how to build reliable systems, while psychology helps me understand how people think, learn, and make decisions. I’m especially interested in technology that connects strong engineering with human needs.

I enjoy building full-stack applications, AI-powered tools, backend systems, and research simulations. Some of the areas I’m most excited about include learning technology, healthcare technology, human-centered product design, reinforcement learning, and intelligent systems.

Across my projects, I care about making complex ideas feel clear, useful, and approachable. Whether I’m working on a scalable search system, an emergency response prototype, or a cognition-inspired simulation, I try to build software that is not only functional, but also thoughtful.
